01 — Problem Context

Where We Are Today

Critical planning data sits across multiple teams with no integration — creating blind spots in material readiness assessment.

📦
Fragmented Data Ownership
Brand/SKU lives with PM, BOM with Engineering, Lead Times with Procurement/ODM, Inventory with Warehouse. No single connected view exists.
⏱️
Lead Time Not BOM-Based
Lead times are provided at a general product level, not component level. Critical long-lead components are missed until it's too late.
🔗
ODM Dependency Gap
Imported/ODM-controlled components lack internal visibility. Sourcing decisions sit outside direct control, making accurate lead time analysis difficult.
🔄
Reactive Procurement
Without early visibility, procurement escalations become reactive. This creates risk of line stoppages and idle manpower.
🚫
No Logistics Loop
PPC proceeds with planning without consulting logistics — a key gap that impacts dispatch and delivery planning accuracy.
💰
Business Impact
Delivery delays → customer dissatisfaction → financial target discrepancies → revenue impact. The problem cascades from planning all the way to business performance.

05 — Planning Concept

Rolling Material Planning

When Month 1 production is active, materials for Month 2 & 3 must already be secured. Month 4 procurement planning initiates at Month 1 midpoint.

Month Primary Activity Material Status Planning Action Readiness
M1 🏭 Active Production
In hand
 Monitor consumption rate. Trigger M4 plan at 50% completion. ● Secure
M2 📦 Materials Secured
Already available
 Production planning confirmed. Line schedule locked. ● Secure
M3 📦 Materials Secured
In transit / warehouse
 Confirm delivery. Resolve any component gaps before M1 midpoint. ● Monitor
M4 📋 Procurement Planning
Orders being placed
 Orders placed when M1 reaches 50% completion. Long-lead items prioritised first. ● Planning
💡 Key Principle
This rolling approach ensures continuous material flow and prevents production stoppages. Lead time of critical components must be considered from the start — not after a shortage occurs.

06 — Improvement Methodology

DMAIC Framework

Following Six Sigma's structured DMAIC approach to systematically eliminate the planning visibility gap. Control phase will be developed over time.

D
Define

Lack of SKU-wise BOM & lead time visibility causing:

  • Production delays
  • Material shortages
  • Delivery delays to customers
  • Idle manpower from line stoppages
  • Financial target discrepancies
  • Overall business impact
M
Measure

Collect and measure:

  • Complete BOM data per SKU
  • Component-level lead times
  • Current inventory levels
  • Planning delay frequency
  • Procurement escalations
  • Material shortage instances
A
Analyze

Root causes identified:

  • BOM not integrated with planning
  • Lead time not component-wise
  • ODM dependency creates blindspots
  • Fragmented data ownership
  • Logistics not in planning loop
  • No centralized dashboard
I
Improve

Solutions to implement:

  • SKU → BOM → LT mapping system
  • Centralized visibility dashboard
  • Risk alerts for unavailable SKUs
  • Lead time improvement process
  • SAP integration (from Apr 1)
  • Logistics included in planning
C
Control

To be developed over time:

  • Regular data update cycles
  • Standardized reporting cadence
  • Exception-based alerts
  • Still being analyzed...
